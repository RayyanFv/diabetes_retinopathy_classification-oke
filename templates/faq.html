Frequently Asked Questions

Diabetic retinopathy is a diabetes complication that affects eyes. It's caused by damage to the blood vessels of the light-sensitive tissue at the back of the eye (retina).

Diabetic retinopathy is diagnosed through a comprehensive eye examination. This includes a visual acuity test, a dilated eye exam, and imaging tests of the retina.

Yes, diabetic retinopathy can be treated. Treatment options include laser surgery, vitrectomy, and injection of medications into the eye. Early detection and treatment can reduce the risk of vision loss.

To prevent diabetic retinopathy, control your blood sugar, blood pressure, and cholesterol levels. Have a comprehensive dilated eye exam at least once a year. Quit smoking and follow your doctor's instructions for managing diabetes.
